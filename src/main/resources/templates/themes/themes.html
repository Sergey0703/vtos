Letter from Elizabeth: Assessment Task 1
CHANGING HOUSE In this extract, Lisa Newman says goodbye to her old house, they move to a new area. The girl recalls her life and imagines how the new owners will settle down. "There will be no more children here!" - Lisa stood in the room and her voice echoed sadly and angry throughout the empty house. For many years she lived in this room with her older sister Mona. And today they were going to go away and leave it. Lisa carefully examined everything around. She saw it for the last time. The blue curtain hanging in the window reminded her of how she fell asleep for hundreds of nights under her quiet flapping. Liza sadly said goodbye to her room. She went into every room to say goodbye to them. They all looked lonely. It was noisy in the house that morning. Workers carried out furniture, carpets and loaded everything into two vans. When it was all over, the house reminded Lisa of a lonely abandoned shell. Soon the new tenants will repaint and renovate everything. Not a single trace of the Newman family will remain in the house: a stain on the floor, traces of plasticine on the shelf, marks on the door of the growth of children and many other signs of a family that has lived here for many years. It was sad for Lisa that everything had to change. The house was bought by an elderly couple - a doctor and his wife. They were wealthy people. How quiet the house will soon become - old feet will walk on thick carpets and stairs, the door will no longer slam. The servants will silently set the table for breakfast, lunch and dinner.
